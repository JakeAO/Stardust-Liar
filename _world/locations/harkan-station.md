# Harkan Station

**Location:** Sector 7, Primary Trade Hub

**Population:** ~50,000 permanent, 10,000+ transient

**Control:** Harkan Syndicate

**Reputation:** *"Expensive, corrupt, and absolutely essential."*

## Overview

Harkan Station is the beating heart of Sector 7 commerce. Every trade route passes through here. Every major transaction happens here. And the Harkan Syndicate controls every credit that changes hands.

The station is a paradox: dangerous but orderly, corrupt but reliable. The syndicate runs things with brutal efficiency. As long as you pay your fees and follow their rules, you can do business here. Cross them, and you'll wish you'd stayed in the outer sectors.

## Districts

### The Docks (Levels 1-8)
- 24 major berths for cargo vessels
- Syndicate enforcers at every checkpoint
- Docking fee: 500 credits minimum, more for larger ships
- Smuggling is tolerated if you pay extra for "privacy"

### The Market Quarter (Levels 9-15)
- Legal merchants selling standard goods (with syndicate licensing fees)
- Gray-market dealers in the back alleys
- Black market access for those with the right connections
- Protection fees apply to everyone

### Residential Blocks (Levels 16-25)
- Housing for station workers and permanent residents
- Surprisingly well-maintained (syndicate keeps infrastructure running)
- Cheap apartments, expensive safety
- Monthly rent includes mandatory "protection fees"

### The Crown (Levels 26-30)
- Syndicate headquarters and administrative offices
- High-end establishments for wealthy clientele
- Invitation-only areas
- Where real power brokers make deals

## The Reality

Life on Harkan Station:
- **Expensive:** Everything costs 20-40% more than it should
- **Orderly:** Crime is low because the syndicate runs everything efficiently
- **Corrupt:** Every official can be bribed, and most expect it
- **Essential:** If you operate in this sector, you deal with Harkan Station

## Syndicate Presence

Enforcers are everywhere:
- Docking bay inspections (looking for "unreported cargo")
- Market patrols (collecting protection fees)
- Residential security (surprisingly effective)
- Crown district guards (do not test them)
